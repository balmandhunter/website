About Me

I specialize in low-cost air pollution measurements. While working on my PhD, I designed and built low-cost flux chambers that measure ozone and carbon dioxide fluxes, validated their performance, and participated in the Front Range Air Pollution and Photochemistry Experiment (FRAPPE). The scope of my project includes computational fluid dynamics modeling (FLUENT), mechanical design (SolidWorks) and construction, electrical construction and debugging, several multiple-week field campaigns, and data analysis (python). Currently, I'm working on exploring techniques for improving calibration and understanding of low-cost ozone sensors, using various statistical methods including machine learning.
